Most small business owners don't find out their website is a problem until someone
tells them, and by then it's usually cost them a few customers already. Here are the
five most common, checkable signs that a redesign is worth looking into — not vague
feelings, things you can actually verify in a few minutes.

## 1. It's slow to load

If your homepage takes more than a couple of seconds to become usable on a phone,
visitors are already leaving before they see what you offer. Speed isn't a nice-to-have
— it's one of the first things both visitors and search engines judge a site on.

## 2. It doesn't work properly on mobile

Most visitors to a small business site arrive on a phone, not a desktop. Pinch-to-zoom
navigation, buttons too small to tap accurately, or a menu that doesn't open correctly
on mobile are all signs the site was designed desktop-first and mobile was an
afterthought.

## 3. There's no clear next step

Open your homepage and ask: what is a visitor supposed to do next? If the answer isn't
obvious within a few seconds — call, book, get a quote, buy — visitors won't hunt for
it. They'll leave instead.

## 4. Your contact information is hard to find

If someone has to dig through a footer or a separate "Contact" page just to find a
phone number or email address, that's friction that costs you leads who were ready to
reach out right then.

## 5. It doesn't show up in search

If you can't find your own business by searching for what you do plus your city, your
potential customers probably can't either. That usually points to missing on-page SEO
basics — page titles, meta descriptions, and site structure — rather than anything
complicated.
