If you've asked a few freelancers or agencies for a quote, you've probably noticed the
numbers are all over the place — a few hundred euros from one person, several thousand
from another, for what sounds like the same project. That range isn't random. It comes
down to a handful of factors that actually drive cost, and once you know what they are,
a quote is a lot easier to evaluate.

## What actually changes the price

**Page count.** A single landing page with a contact form is a fundamentally smaller
job than a 5-7 page site with a blog, service pages, and a content management system
behind it. Most pricing tiers exist because of this one variable more than any other.

**Custom design vs. a template.** A site built around your business, your content, and
your goals takes more design and development time than filling in a pre-made template —
but it also won't look like every other small business site using the same theme.

**CMS and ongoing content needs.** If you want to publish blog posts or update pages
yourself after launch, that requires a content-management setup, not just a static
page. That's a real feature, not a checkbox, and it affects scope.

**SEO and technical foundation.** A site that's fast, mobile-friendly, and structured
correctly for search engines from day one costs more up front than one that isn't — but
it also doesn't need to be rebuilt six months later when the lack of SEO catches up with
it.

**Support after launch.** Some quotes are launch-only. Others include a window of
post-launch fixes and small updates. That's worth confirming either way before you
compare two numbers.

## What it typically costs

Freelance and small-agency rates for a custom small business website generally range
from a few hundred euros for a single-page site up to a few thousand for a multi-page,
CMS-ready build — with plenty of variation depending on where the freelancer is based
and what's actually included.
