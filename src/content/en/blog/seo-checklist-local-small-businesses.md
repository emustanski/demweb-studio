Search engine optimization sounds technical, but most of what actually matters for a
local small business is straightforward — it's just easy to skip if nobody's checked.
Here's the practical order to work through it.

## 1. Claim and complete your Google Business Profile

This is the single highest-impact thing most local businesses haven't fully done.
Accurate hours, address, phone number, category, and photos directly affect whether you
show up in local map results — often before your actual website does.

## 2. Keep your name, address, and phone number consistent everywhere

Your business details should match exactly across your website, Google Business
Profile, and any directory listings. Small inconsistencies (a suite number here, an
abbreviation there) can quietly undermine local search trust.

## 3. Write page titles and descriptions that mention your service and location

A page titled "Home" tells search engines nothing. A page titled "Plumbing Repairs in
[Your City]" tells them exactly what you do and where — and it's the difference between
showing up for a relevant local search or not.

## 4. Make sure your site is fast and mobile-friendly

Search engines factor in page speed and mobile usability directly. A slow or
poorly-formatted mobile site isn't just a user-experience problem — it's an SEO one
too.

## 5. Structure your content with proper headings

A logical heading structure (one clear H1, organized H2s underneath) helps search
engines understand what a page is actually about, not just what it says.

## 6. Get a handful of real reviews

Reviews on your Google Business Profile are a genuine local-ranking factor, not just
social proof. A small, steady trickle of real reviews outperforms a one-time push for
dozens.

## 7. Fix broken links and missing alt text

Small technical issues — broken internal links, images with no alt text — add up. None
of them individually tank your ranking, but together they signal a site that isn't
well-maintained.
